Bhagat singh

Bhagat Singh (27 September 1907[a] – 23 March 1931) was a charismatic Indian revolutionary.

About the Legend

☛Bhagat Singh was born on 27 September 1907[a] in the village of Banga in the Lyallpur district of the Punjab in what was then British India and is today Pakistan.

☛ HE took part in a largely symbolic bombing of the Central Legislative Assembly in Delhi and a hunger strike in jail, which—on the back of sympathetic coverage in Indian-owned newspapers—turned him into a household name in the Punjab region, and after his execution at age 23 into a martyr and folk hero in Northern India.

☛ He is sometimes get referred to as the Shaheed-e-Azam ("Great martyr" in Urdu and Punjabi)hE

☛ In 1928 he took part in a plot to kill the police chief responsible for the death of influential Indian writer and politician Lala Lajpat Rai.

☛ His slogan was "“They may kill me, but they cannot kill my ideas. They can crush my body, but they will not be able to crush my spirit.””

☛ In 1931 Bhagat Singh was hanged for the murder of Officer J.P. Saunders in the city of Lahore (then in India).
